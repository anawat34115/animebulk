Here's something most people miss: almost every iconic anime character trains with **bodyweight movements**. Tanjiro runs up mountains. Rock Lee does thousands of push-ups and sit-ups. Naruto sprints through forests. None of them have a gym membership.

Calisthenics — bodyweight training — is the original anime training method. And it works.

## Why Calisthenics Builds Anime Bodies

Calisthenics develops the qualities that make anime characters look the way they do:

- **Relative strength** — strong for your bodyweight, which means lean and functional
- **Body control** — the ability to move your body through space with precision
- **Muscular endurance** — the capacity to keep going, like Naruto in a 100-episode arc
- **Aesthetics** — calisthenics builds the V-taper, defined abs, and athletic proportions that anime characters have

## The Big 6 Calisthenics Movements

Master these six movements and you have everything you need:

### 1. Push-Up
**Muscles:** Chest, shoulders, triceps, core

The foundation. Start here, progress to archer push-ups, then one-arm push-ups.

**Progression path:**
- Wall push-ups → Incline push-ups → Standard push-ups → Decline push-ups → Archer push-ups → One-arm push-ups

### 2. Pull-Up
**Muscles:** Back, biceps, core

The single best upper body exercise. If you can't do one yet, start with dead hangs and negative pull-ups.

**Progression path:**
- Dead hang → Negative pull-ups → Band-assisted pull-ups → Full pull-ups → Weighted pull-ups → Muscle-up

### 3. Squat
**Muscles:** Quads, hamstrings, glutes, core

**Progression path:**
- Bodyweight squat → Jump squat → Bulgarian split squat → Pistol squat

### 4. Hinge (Hip Hinge)
**Muscles:** Hamstrings, glutes, lower back

**Progression path:**
- Good morning → Single-leg deadlift → Nordic curl

### 5. Core (Plank / Hollow Body)
**Muscles:** Entire core, shoulder stability

**Progression path:**
- Plank → Hollow body hold → L-sit → Dragon flag

### 6. Dip
**Muscles:** Chest, triceps, shoulders

**Progression path:**
- Bench dip → Parallel bar dip → Weighted dip → Ring dip

## The Anime Calisthenics Starter Program

**4 days per week. No equipment needed except a pull-up bar.**

### Day A — Push Focus

| Exercise | Sets | Reps |
|----------|------|------|
| Push-Ups | 4 | 15-20 |
| Pike Push-Ups | 3 | 10-12 |
| Tricep Dips (chair) | 3 | 12-15 |
| Plank | 3 | 45 sec |
| Mountain Climbers | 3 | 20 each |

### Day B — Pull Focus

| Exercise | Sets | Reps |
|----------|------|------|
| Pull-Ups (or negatives) | 4 | 6-10 |
| Inverted Rows | 3 | 12 |
| Dead Hang | 3 | 30 sec |
| Chin-Ups | 3 | 8 |
| Bicep Curls (resistance band) | 3 | 15 |

### Day C — Legs & Core

| Exercise | Sets | Reps |
|----------|------|------|
| Bodyweight Squats | 4 | 20 |
| Jump Squats | 3 | 12 |
| Bulgarian Split Squat | 3 | 10 each |
| Glute Bridge | 3 | 15 |
| Hollow Body Hold | 3 | 20 sec |
| Leg Raises | 3 | 12 |

### Day D — Full Body Circuit

**3 rounds, 30 sec rest between exercises:**

1. Burpees × 10
2. Pull-Ups × 6
3. Push-Ups × 15
4. Jump Squats × 12
5. Dips × 10
6. Plank × 45 sec

## How to Progress

The biggest mistake in calisthenics is staying at the same level too long. Here's how to know when to progress:

**Move to the next progression when you can complete 3 sets of the top rep range with good form and 2 minutes of rest.**

For example: once you can do 3×20 push-ups cleanly, move to archer push-ups.
