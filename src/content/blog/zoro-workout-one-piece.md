Roronoa Zoro lifts weights with his mouth. He trains while injured. He sleeps anywhere, trains everywhere, and has one singular goal that he pursues with absolute obsession. His physique reflects that obsession: **broad, thick, powerful muscle** with a shredded midsection that looks like it was carved by a swordsmith.

Zoro is the anime embodiment of what happens when you combine heavy compound lifting with insane training volume and an unbreakable will.

## Zoro's Physical Profile

Zoro sits at approximately 5'11" and 185 lbs at around 10% body fat. His defining physical characteristics:

- **Massive shoulders and traps** — from constant overhead and pulling work
- **Thick chest** — built for power generation in sword strikes
- **Powerful forearms and grip** — three swords demand elite grip strength
- **Defined, strong core** — the rotational foundation of every technique
- **Proportional legs** — often hidden by his pants, but critical for his stance and power

This is a **strength-hypertrophy hybrid physique** — not a bodybuilder's aesthetic, but a fighter's functional mass.

## The Two Pillars of Zoro's Training

### Pillar 1: Heavy Compound Lifting

Zoro's strength comes from moving heavy loads through full ranges of motion. The big five:

| Movement | Primary Muscles | Zoro's Target |
|----------|----------------|---------------|
| Deadlift | Full posterior chain | 1.8× bodyweight |
| Back Squat | Quads, glutes, core | 1.5× bodyweight |
| Bench Press | Chest, shoulders, triceps | 1.3× bodyweight |
| Overhead Press | Shoulders, triceps, core | 0.8× bodyweight |
| Weighted Pull-Up | Back, biceps | +25kg × 5 reps |

These are the benchmarks of a Zoro-level physique. Work toward them progressively over months and years.

### Pillar 2: Extreme Volume

Zoro's signature training element is **volume**. He doesn't just lift heavy — he does thousands of reps. The famous 3,000-rep training sessions aren't realistic for natural humans, but the principle is: **high volume builds the dense, full muscle bellies that give Zoro his look.**

In practice, this means:
- Higher rep ranges on accessory work (12–20 reps)
- More sets per muscle group per week (16–20 sets)
- One dedicated high-volume day per week (the "Zoro Special")

## Building Zoro's Shoulders and Traps

The most visually defining part of Zoro's physique is his upper body width. Here's how to build it:

### Shoulder Width (Lateral Deltoids)
- Dumbbell lateral raises: 4 × 15–20, controlled tempo
- Cable lateral raises: 3 × 20 (constant tension)
- Upright rows: 3 × 12

### Trap Thickness
- Barbell shrugs: 4 × 15 with heavy weight
- Farmer's carries: 3 × 40m (traps work isometrically the entire time)
- Face pulls: 3 × 20 (also protects shoulder health)

### Rear Delts (Critical for the 3D shoulder look)
- Reverse pec deck: 3 × 20
- Band pull-aparts: 3 × 25 daily
- Face pulls: already listed above — do them every session

## Nutrition: Eating Like the Future World's Greatest

Zoro needs to fuel both heavy strength work and high-volume sessions. This requires a genuine caloric surplus.

**Caloric target:** Bodyweight (lbs) × 18

**Macros:**
- Protein: 1g per lb (muscle repair from high volume)
- Carbs: 2.5g per lb (fuel for both strength and volume sessions)
- Fats: 0.4g per lb

## Sleep: Zoro's Secret Weapon

Zoro is famous for sleeping anywhere, anytime. This isn't a joke — it's his recovery strategy. **Sleep is when muscle protein synthesis peaks, growth hormone releases, and neural adaptations consolidate.**

Target 8–9 hours per night. If you can't get that, add a 20-minute nap. Zoro would.
